## Story Arc — Five Beats

### Beat 1 — The Failed Promise (~60s)

*Open on hero page. Let the shield animation play. Then:*

> The enterprise AI race is real. The AI agents market hit $7.63 billion in 2025. 85% of enterprises have committed to AI agent deployment.
>
> And yet — MIT reported in August 2025 that 95% of generative AI pilots at companies are failing. Gartner warns that 40% of AI agent projects risk cancellation by 2027.
>
> Not because the technology doesn't work. But because enterprises keep running into five walls — and no existing platform solves all five at once.

---

### Beat 2 — The Five Walls (~90s)

*Slow cursor movement across the hero section as each wall is named. No scrolling — stay on hero.*

> **Wall 1: Your data has to leave your environment.**
>
> Azure, AWS, Google, Salesforce — every major cloud AI platform requires your data to exit your perimeter. For regulated industries — financial services, healthcare, government defense — that isn't a trade-off. That is a hard stop. And yes, local model runners like Ollama keep data private. But they have no orchestration, no governance, no institutional memory. A model runner is not an enterprise platform.

---

> **Wall 2: You are locked into one provider — or one cloud.**
>
> 37% of enterprises now run five or more LLM models in production. In June 2025, two major OpenAI outages paralyzed AI-dependent workflows at thousands of organizations. Single-provider AI is now a business continuity risk. And yet every major platform pulls you deeper: Microsoft to Azure OpenAI, Salesforce to Salesforce cloud, Snowflake to Snowflake's perimeter. Switching costs everything.

---

> **Wall 3: Costs are completely unpredictable.**
>
> 53% of AI teams see costs exceed their forecasts by 40% or more. 84% report margin erosion. Only 15% of organizations can forecast AI costs accurately. Most platforms abstract cost until the invoice arrives. By then it's too late, and the budget conversation is already uncomfortable.

---

> **Wall 4: AI is a black box — and regulators have noticed.**
>
> The EU AI Act enforcement began in 2025. Italy fined OpenAI €15 million for GDPR violations. The FTC launched Operation AI Comply. Regulators now require documented audit trails for every AI interaction — who ran it, what data it touched, what it decided, when, and why. Black box AI is no longer compliant AI.

---

> **Wall 5: Discovery never becomes production.**
>
> Every team that discovers an insight conversationally has to rebuild it as a production API — then maintain both — then watch them diverge. The handoff from AI exploration to AI operationalization costs weeks, introduces errors, and nobody catches the drift until something breaks in production.

---

### Beat 3 — The Promise (~30s)

*Draw cursor slowly to the headline:* **"Cloud-Level Reasoning. Zero-Trust Privacy."**

> Uderia was designed to knock down all five walls — simultaneously. Not to compromise between intelligence and privacy. Not to commit to one LLM. Not to hide cost until the bill arrives. Not to be a black box. Not to force a rebuild. To solve all five — in one platform.

---

### Beat 4 — Three Pillars (~45s)

*Walk each pill in turn:*

**Zero-Trust Sovereignty**
> Your data never has to move. You decide what executes where. Cloud AI for strategic planning. Local models for sensitive execution. Both at the same time. The sovereign cloud market is growing from $154 billion today to $823 billion by 2032 — enterprises are demanding exactly this. Uderia is built for it.

**Cloud-Level Intelligence**
> Choosing privacy doesn't mean choosing a weaker model. Uderia uses Champion Cases — proven strategies from your organization's own execution history — to teach local models to perform with the sophistication of a cloud hyperscaler. You get the reasoning. Your data stays home.

**Extreme Efficiency**
> Every successful query becomes institutional memory. The platform learns from itself. It gets faster, more accurate, and cheaper with every execution. And every token, every model call, every turn is tracked — with actual costs, not estimates — in real time.

---

### Beat 5 — The Shield Badge Close (~20s)

*Cursor or zoom to the animated shield:* **BUILT FOR TERADATA — OPEN FOR THE WORLD**

> Uderia was born in the Teradata ecosystem — one of the most demanding enterprise data environments on the planet. The problems it solves — sovereignty, compliance, cost, lock-in, operationalization — are universal. That is why it is open to every enterprise. And open source, so you own it.
